Chapter III

The Stolen Body

A funeral route diverted by power.

From Babylon to Egypt

Alexander's body was meant to return to Macedonia. But Ptolemy, one of his generals, intercepted it and brought it to Egypt instead. That decision was both symbolic and strategic.

First the body was taken to Memphis. Later it was moved again to Alexandria, the city Alexander had founded. Egypt became the keeper of his remains and, with them, a piece of his authority.
