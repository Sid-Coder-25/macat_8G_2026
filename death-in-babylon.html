Chapter II

Death in Babylon

A sudden ending that opened a larger mystery.

An empire left without a clear heir

In 323 BCE, Alexander died suddenly in Babylon. He was only 32. His empire was enormous, but there was no clear leader ready to control it.

His generals quickly turned against one another. The struggle was not just about land or armies. Even Alexander's body became political. Whoever controlled his tomb controlled part of his legend.

His death ended the march of an empire, but began the mystery of a tomb the world still searches for.
