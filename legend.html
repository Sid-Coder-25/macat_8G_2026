Chapter I

The Legend

The ruler who became more than a man.

The king who changed the ancient world

Alexander became king at just 20. By 32, he had defeated Persia, entered Egypt, founded cities, and changed the ancient world forever. His campaigns stretched from Greece to the edges of India.

He was not remembered only as a ruler. He became a symbol of power, ambition, and near-mythic greatness. That is why even his tomb carried enormous meaning long after his death.

Even in death, Alexander left behind one final conquest: the mystery of his lost tomb.
